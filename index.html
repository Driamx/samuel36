Services

our dishes are a delicious and versatile part of the country's culinary tradition, offering a rich variety of flavors, textures, and experiences. From the hearty warmth of ramen and udon to the light, refreshing taste of cold soba, each noodle type reflects deep-rooted culture and craftsmanship. These dishes combine umami-rich broths, fresh toppings, and handmade noodles to create meals that are comforting, balanced, and satisfying. Whether hot or cold, stir-fried or in broth, Japanese noodles are a beloved comfort food enjoyed worldwide for their depth of flavor and simple elegance. !

Tonkotsu Ramen

Broth: Creamy pork bone broth Noodles: Thin, straight ramen noodles Toppings: Chashu (braised pork belly), ajitama (seasoned egg), green onions, black garlic oil, kikurage mushrooms

Zaru Soba

Noodles: Cold buckwheat soba noodles Dipping Sauce: Tsuyu (soy-based dipping sauce) with wasabi, green onions, and grated daikon Toppings (optional): Nori (shredded seaweed), tempura on the side Accompaniments: Green tea, pickled vegetables

Yaki Udon

Noodles: Thick udon noodles stir-fried Sauce: Soy sauce, mirin, and sesame oil blend Ingredients: Sliced beef or chicken, cabbage, carrots, green onions Toppings: Bonito flakes, sesame seeds

Kitsune Udon

Broth: Light soy-based dashi Noodles: Thick, chewy udon noodles Toppings: Aburaage (sweet fried tofu), green onions, kamaboko (fish cake) Accompaniments: Side of onigiri (rice ball)
